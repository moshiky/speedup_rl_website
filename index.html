Leveraging Human Knowledge in Tabular Reinforcement Learning

Reinforcement Learning (RL) can be extremely effective in solving complex, real-world problems. However, injecting human knowledge into an RL agent may require extensive effort on the human designer’s part. To date, human factors are generally not considered in the development and evaluation of possible approaches. In this project, we propose and evaluate a novel method, based on human psychology literature, and compare State Of The Art speedup methods using a first of its kind human study.

Better utilizing human knowledge in speeding up reinforcement learning

The SASS Approach

According to constructivism literature, and specifically personal construct psychology [Kelly, 1955], while designing and testing an RL agent, the human designer himself learns the traits of the domain at hand by identifying patterns and domain-specific characteristics. To accommodate both prior knowledge and learned insights (which may change over time), it is necessary to allow the designer to easily explore and refine different similarity hypotheses.

Non-Expert Study

We amid at evaluating the 'real' effectiveness and efficiency of State Of The Art RL speedup methods by examining the performance of large number of programmers with average programming skills and basic understanding of the RL principles.
Prior to the experiment, all participants introduced with a tutorial reminding them of the basics of Q-learning and explaining task’s specification. Participants attended three meetings, a week apart, in which they implemented their domain-specific knowledge how to accelerate the RL agent learning by using a different speedup method each time.
The participants requested to accelerate the learning of on one of three domains- the Simple Robotic Soccer (simple), Pursuit (a bit harder) and MarioAI (very complex) games.
